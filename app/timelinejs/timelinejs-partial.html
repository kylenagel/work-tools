Timeline JS

Link: Timeline JS
What it does: Timeline JS, built by the Knight Lab at Northwestern University, creates slick interactive timelines from information stored in a Google Spreadsheet. Those timelines can be embedded on our websites.

How to make one

1. Before starting, confirm or set up a Google account

  • This is important because, not only are Google accounts very useful for creating and storing all kinds of Google products and information, but also because a Google Spreadsheet is needed to create the timeline.
  • Go to drive.google.com.
  • If you already have a Google account (a gmail address), sign in. If not, create one. It's free and easy. Just follow the instructions.

2. Create a blank Google Spreadsheet

  • Open your drive, drive.google.com.
  • Click the red "CREATE" button on the left and then "Spreadsheet."
  • Rename the spreadsheet to something that will be easy to find later. File > Rename.

3. Copy the header row from the Timeline JS template

  • Open the Timeline JS template
  • Copy the header row into the first row of your new spreadsheet.
  • Descriptions for what the column headers mean are at the bottom of the template spreadsheet.

4. Fill out your spreadsheet

  • Descriptions for what the column headers mean are at the bottom of the template spreadsheet.
  • Many questions about input are answered in the "Help" section of the Timeline JS site, at the bottom.

5. Publish your spreadsheet

  • File > Publish to the web
  • Click Publish (yes, when you're asked, you're sure you want to publish this selection).

6. Preview the timeline

  • Go back to the Timeline JS site.
  • Go to Step 3, midway down the page.
  • Paste the url of your now-completed Google Spreadsheet into the box.
  • Go to Step 4 on the site and click Preview.

7. Get the embed code

  • When the timeline is done, the embed code you need is in the gray box on Step 4.
