Chartbuilder

Link: Chartbuilder
What it does: Chartbuilder, built by a developer for the news site Quartz, allows you to paste your tabbed data into a window, choose from some limited options and output a static chart as an image. It makes a nice chart, but the size and the options are sometimes frustratingly limited.

How to make one

1. Organize your data

  • Include a header row.
  • The first column will be the X-axis information. The remaining columns will be their own lines/bars/dot sets in the chart.
  • An Excel file works well, because that data can be copied and pasted into the window.
  • Tabbed data from a text editor will also work.

2. Paste the data into the window

  • Copy all of it out of the spreadsheet or text fiile and paste it into the window at step 1.
  • Remove commas and other characters (like dollar signs) from your data.

3. Format your chart

  • To change colors of the lines, dots or bars, click on the color square in step 2 and choose a new color.
  • Pick a chart type
    • Line: Line chart
    • Column: Columns facing up
    • Bar Grid: Columns facing to the side
    • Scatter: Unconnected dots
  • In step 3, add a title to the chart if one is needed. You can also add credit and source information.

4. Export the chart as an image

  • At the bottom, click the button, "Create Image of Chart."
  • Choose the first option, "Download Image of Chart."
